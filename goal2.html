Goal 2: Zero Hunger

End hunger, achieve food security and improved nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture

The number of undernourished people has dropped by almost half in the past two decades due to rapid economic growth and increased agricultural productivity. Many developing countries that used to suffer from famine and hunger can now meet their nutritional needs.

Regions such as Central and East Asia, Latin America, and the Caribbean have made substantial progress in eradicating extreme hunger. However, extreme hunger and malnutrition remain significant barriers to development in many areas.

As of 2017, 821 million people are estimated to be chronically undernourished, often due to environmental degradation, drought, and biodiversity loss. Over 90 million children under five are dangerously underweight, and food insecurity is increasing in almost all regions of Africa and South America.

The Sustainable Development Goals aim to end all forms of hunger and malnutrition by 2030, ensuring that all people—especially children—have sufficient and nutritious food year-round. This involves promoting sustainable agriculture, supporting small-scale farmers, and ensuring equal access to resources like land, technology, and markets.
